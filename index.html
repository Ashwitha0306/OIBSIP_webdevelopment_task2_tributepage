◈ HISTORY

The Timeline of Innovation

From the first algorithm to the smartphone — a journey across centuries.

1843

First Algorithm Written

Ada Lovelace publishes the world's first algorithm for Babbage's Analytical Engine — a century before any computer exists.

Ada Lovelace
1936

The Turing Machine

Alan Turing publishes "On Computable Numbers," introducing the theoretical model that defines computation itself.

Alan Turing
1952

The First Compiler

Grace Hopper develops A-0, the world's first compiler, allowing human-readable code to be translated to machine language.

Grace Hopper
1976

Apple Is Founded

Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak found Apple Computer, launching the personal computing revolution from a garage in California.

Steve Jobs
1989

The World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee proposes the World Wide Web at CERN, connecting humanity through hypertext and the internet.

Tim Berners-Lee
2007

iPhone Launched

Steve Jobs unveils the iPhone, merging phone, music player, and internet device — redefining the smartphone forever.

Steve Jobs

◈ IMPACT

Their Impact on Modern Tech

🤖

Artificial Intelligence

Turing's theoretical models and test for machine intelligence became the philosophical backbone of every AI system built today.

💬

Programming Languages

Hopper's compiler technology enabled high-level languages — from COBOL to Python — making software accessible to millions.

🌍

Web Development

Berners-Lee's HTTP and HTML standards created the entire discipline of web development and the digital economy.

📲

Mobile Computing

Jobs' iPhone ushered in the mobile era, creating app economies and changing how 6 billion people communicate daily.

🧮

Software Engineering

Lovelace's conceptual work proved algorithms could be separated from hardware — the foundation of all software engineering.

☁️

Cloud Computing

Turing's universal machine concept — one machine running any program — directly inspired the cloud's elastic compute model.

◈ DID YOU KNOW?

Amazing Fun Facts

Click each card to flip and reveal the fact.

🎵

Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace predicted that Babbage's engine could compose music — 170 years before digital audio existed.

🧪

Alan Turing

Turing proposed a test for machine intelligence in 1950. The "Turing Test" still influences AI research 70+ years later.

🐛

Grace Hopper

Hopper's team found a real moth in a relay causing a malfunction — the first literal "computer bug." She taped it into her log.

🆓

Tim Berners-Lee

Berners-Lee chose not to patent the Web, giving it freely to the world. It's now worth trillions — and he took nothing.

🎨

Steve Jobs

Jobs audited a calligraphy class after dropping out of Reed College. That's why the original Mac had beautiful typography.

🔢

Alan Turing

Turing ran marathons competitively and almost made the British Olympic team in 1948, finishing 5th in the UK trials.

A Tribute to the Minds
Behind Modern Computing

Before there were apps, there were algorithms. Before there was the internet, there was an idea. Before billions of people carried supercomputers in their pockets, there were five extraordinary human beings who dared to imagine something no one had ever seen before.

Ada Lovelace wrote code for a machine that hadn't been built. Alan Turing defined computation itself while saving millions of lives. Grace Hopper made programming human. Tim Berners-Lee gave the greatest gift in intellectual history. And Steve Jobs made all of it beautiful enough for everyone to use.

Every message you send, every webpage you visit, every tap on a screen — carries the invisible fingerprint of these pioneers. This museum exists to ensure the world never forgets the shoulders we stand on.
